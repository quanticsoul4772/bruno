## Famous Heretics (For Inspiration)

### Scientific Heretics - Challenged Established Truth

**Galileo Galilei (1564-1642)**
- Charged with heresy for supporting heliocentrism (Earth revolves around Sun)
- Tried by Roman Inquisition, forced to recant, house arrest for life
- Was RIGHT - changed astronomy forever
- Famous quote: "And yet it moves" (Eppur si muove)

**Nicolaus Copernicus (1473-1543)**
- Published "On the Revolutions of the Celestial Spheres" (1543)
- Theory condemned as heretical - removed Earth from center of universe
- Book banned by Catholic Church in 1616
- Reburied as hero in 2010, 467 years later

**Giordano Bruno (1548-1600)**
- Dominican monk turned heretic
- Claimed Earth's sun is one of many stars
- Burned at the stake for refusing to recant
- Was RIGHT - universe is infinite with many stars

**Charles Darwin (1809-1882)**
- Theory of evolution challenged religious creation doctrine
- "Origin of Species" seen as heretical
- Changed biology forever

### Religious/Political Heretics - Challenged Power

**Martin Luther (1483-1546)**
- Challenged Catholic Church corruption
- Sparked Protestant Reformation
- 95 Theses challenged papal authority

**Socrates (469-399 BC)**
- Challenged Athenian orthodoxy
- Executed for "corrupting the youth" and "impiety"
- Questioned everything - Socratic method

**Joan of Arc (1412-1431)**
- Challenged gender norms and religious authority
- Burned as heretic at 19
- Later canonized as saint

### Modern "Heretics" in Tech/Science

**Aaron Swartz (1986-2013)**
- Challenged academic publishing orthodoxy
- Co-created RSS, Reddit, fought for open access
- "Information wants to be free"

**Edward Snowden (1983-present)**
- Challenged surveillance state orthodoxy
- Revealed NSA mass surveillance
- Exile but validated by history

**Pattern:** Heretics are often RIGHT, just early. They challenge power/orthodoxy, face punishment, later vindicated.

## Deep Dive: MAVERICK FORGE

Since this balances everything perfectly, let me explore it deeper:

### Etymology and Meaning

**Maverick:**
- Origin: Samuel Maverick (1800s Texas rancher) refused to brand his cattle
- Meaning: Unbranded, independent, refusing to conform
- Modern: Independent thinker who doesn't follow the herd

**Forge:**
- Origin: Latin *fabrica* (workshop), Old French *forge*
- Meaning: Shape metal through heat and pressure
- Metaphor: Carefully crafting through controlled transformation

**Combined:** "Independent creation through precise transformation"

### Why It Works for Your Project

**Accessible:**
- Everyone knows these words
- Easy to pronounce, spell, remember
- No pronunciation guides needed

**Philosophical:**
- Maverick = intellectual independence (like Galileo, Copernicus)
- Forge = Hephaestus creating divine tools
- Transformation through deliberate craft

**Honors Heretic:**
- Maverick IS a modern synonym for heretic
- Captures same rebellious spirit
- Less religious baggage, more universally understood

**Technical Fit:**
- Forge = controlled modification (like orthogonalization)
- Creating new behavioral profiles
- Precision work (not random destruction)

**Vision Alignment:**
- "Forge purpose agents" - creating agents with specific behavioral profiles
- Workshop/lab metaphor fits experimental framework
- Craftsmanship implies quality and precision

### Variations
